# Unit 1: Basics - Island Explorers

## Workshop Overview

**Team size:** Four students  
**Slide source:** Use the named slides/steps from the official LEGO facilitation-note print/PDF pages embedded in the agendas below.

The core workshop combines lessons B101-B104. Hour 1 moves from screen-free route planning into coded sequences. Hour 2 connects hardware and software through colour, movement and dance. After lunch, the optional extension adapts both parts of B105 Island Explorers into a ship-navigation design challenge.

## Team Roles

Use LEGO's four-person collaborative structure. Rotate roles at `1:00`, after lunch and at extension `1:00`.

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| Designer | Plans the route and model. |
| Builder | Leads construction and shares materials. |
| Programmer | Leads Coding Canvas work and explains the sequence. |
| Tester-documenter | Predicts outcomes, runs tests and records revisions. |

## Adapted B105 Rubric

| Criterion | Workshop Target | Stretch |
| --- | --- | --- |
| System design | Identify inputs and outputs and describe the sequence. | Explain how all three work together as a system. |
| Iteration | Make at least one sequence change. | Explain why the revision improves navigation. |
| Roles | Practice multiple roles with rotation. | Rotate independently and help teammates. |
| Prototype | Build a ship and island prototype. | Personalise and improve both. |
